Defination Of Al-Quran

🌙 Light from the Qur’an | A Blog Reflecting on the Words of Allah 📖 Post 1: Surah Al-Fatiha — The Opening of Mercy and Guidance “In the name of Allah, the Most Gracious, the Most Merciful.” (Surah Al-Fatiha, 1:1) 🌟 Reflection: Surah Al-Fatiha is the heart of the Qur’an — recited in every prayer, it reminds us of Allah’s mercy and our constant need for His guidance. "Ar-Rahman" (The Most Gracious) and "Ar-Raheem" (The Most Merciful) are two beautiful names of Allah that show His unmatched kindness and love. Every action should begin with Bismillah, reminding us to seek blessings and sincerity. 🧠 Life Lessons: Always start your tasks with Allah’s name. Be kind and merciful toward others, as Allah is merciful to us. 🙏 Practical Tip: Before eating, studying, or making decisions — say “Bismillah” with intention. It brings blessings and protects your heart. 🔖 Ideas for Future Blog Posts: Surah Ikhlas: The Core of Tawheed (Oneness of God) Ayah of the Day: Short Reflections for Busy Lives Dua and Qur’an: Finding Peace Through Supplication Qur’anic Stories: Lessons from the Lives of Prophets Names of Allah Series: Understanding Al-Ghafoor, Al-Hakeem, etc.

Hadith

Introduction To Hadith

: 🌿 Words of the Prophet ﷺ | A Hadith Reflection Blog 📜 Post 1: Intentions Matter Most “Actions are judged by intentions, and every person will get what they intended.” — Sahih al-Bukhari & Muslim 💡 Reflection: This is one of the most powerful and foundational hadiths in Islam. It teaches us that Allah doesn’t just look at what we do — He looks at why we do it. A good deed with the wrong intention may lose its reward. But a small act, done sincerely for Allah, can carry immense value in the Hereafter. 🧠 Life Lessons: Before praying, giving charity, helping others, or even smiling — ask yourself: "Am I doing this for Allah?" Sincerity (Ikhlas) makes even ordinary actions into acts of worship. Don’t seek praise from people; seek acceptance from Allah. ✅ Try This Today: Choose one action — like helping someone or making dua — and do it only for Allah, without telling anyone. Reflect on how that feels. 🔖 Upcoming Blog Topics: “None of you truly believes…” – The Hadith on Brotherhood The Importance of Good Character in Islam Mercy of the Prophet ﷺ in Hadith How the Prophet ﷺ Treated His Family Short Hadiths to Memorize & Live By

Sunnah

🌟 Living the Sunnah | A Blog on the Way of the Prophet ﷺ 🕊️ Post 1: The Sunnah of Smiling 😊 The Prophet ﷺ said: “Your smile for your brother is a charity.” — [Jami’ at-Tirmidhi] 💡 Reflection: A simple smile — sincere and kind — can be an act of worship in Islam. The Prophet ﷺ was known for his warm, approachable, and cheerful nature. He smiled often and taught his companions to spread joy. Today, in a world full of stress and negativity, reviving this small Sunnah can make a big difference. 🧠 Life Lessons: Sunnah is not just about prayer and fasting — it's also about how we treat others. A smile can uplift someone’s mood and build bonds of love and brotherhood. It's a form of sadaqah (charity) — no money, just sincerity. ✅ Try This Sunnah Today: Make a conscious effort to smile at at least 3 people today — your family, a neighbor, or a stranger — with the intention of following the Sunnah. 📅 Upcoming Blog Ideas: The Sunnah of Eating: Sit, Say Bismillah, and Eat with the Right Hand Sleeping Like the Prophet ﷺ: Evening Sunnahs for Better Rest Morning Routine of the Prophet ﷺ: Duas, Miswak & Gratitude Kindness to Animals: A Forgotten Sunnah Reviving Forgotten Sunnahs in Daily Life
